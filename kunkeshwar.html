Kunkeshwar

Kunakeshwar village Kunkeshwar is a coastal village in Devgad taluka of Sindhudurg district in the Konkan region of Maharashtra, India . It is located 20 kilometres south of Devgad village. Kunkeshwar is a pilgrimage and tourist destination. The village is also famous for its 'Hapus Mangoes'. Weather During the monsoon, there is a lot of rain and the climate remains temperate. In winter, the climate is cold and fog often falls in the morning. In summer, the climate is hot. During the monsoon, rice is cultivated here. Kunakeshwar Temple There is a Shiva temple called Shri Dev Kunakeshwar in Kunakeshwar. Chhatrapati Shivaji himself renovated this temple. Kunakeshwar is called the Kashi of South Konkan. Kunakeshwar Yatra Pillars of the gods during the pilgrimage Every year, a three-day pilgrimage is held to Kunkeshwar on the occasion of Mahashivratri. A big fair is also held in the temple premises on the occasion of this pilgrimage. The Kalingad Bazaar and Malvani Khaje are the special features of this fair. Except for a few exceptions, all the devotees who come to this pilgrimage still come on foot along with their gavarayata. For visiting Kunkeshwar, a boat bridge is built on the Tarammumbari creek for the Dirba-Rameshwar of Jamsande, who come from a distance of 12 km. Every 24 years, the Bhagwati Mata of Kotkamte village, who comes to visit Kunkeshwar, is brought to the Kunkeshwar area by the villagers of that village with enthusiasm, playing drums. The Bhagwati Mata of Munge village, who comes from 16 km., visits Kunkeshwar on foot without taking a rest on the way. Shri Bharateshwar of Masure village, who comes from 50 km., visits Kunkeshwar on foot along with his gavarayata. Also, many gods like Kinjavade-Sthaneshwar, Dabhole-Pavanai, Tembavli-Kavlai, along with their followers, make a pilgrimage to Kunkeshwar on foot. Kunkeshwar Chowpatty There is a famous beach near Kunkeshwar Temple. Lakhs of domestic and foreign tourists visit here every year. Around 100 Shivalingas are carved on the purple stones in the sea. These Shivalingas can be seen during the monsoon season.
