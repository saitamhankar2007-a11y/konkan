Aamboli

Amboli refers to a popular hill station in Maharashtra's Sindhudurg district, known for lush Western Ghats scenery, waterfalls, and biodiversityThe hill station offers nature walks, viewpoints, and rich flora/fauna.Amboli is surrounded by the Sahyadri hill valleys from three sides, while it opens to the Konkan coast at the fourth side. Climate, flora and fauna of Amboli make an enjoyable combination for the tourists. Also, the clean and quiet hills here serve you rich hot biodiversity to cherish in sightseeing.

Kunkeshwar

Kunkeshwar is a place near Devgad and has a very beautiful beach, near the Kunkeshwar temple. This beach is clean, less crowded. You can enjoy the Konkan and the sea here.The Kunakeshwar Temple is a significant cultural and religious landmark. Dating back to the Yadava dynasty, this temple is an architectural marvel adorned with intricate carvings. During Mahashivratri, the temple hosts grand celebrations, attracting devotees from across Maharashtra and beyond. The locals worship Lord Shiva here as “Kunakeshwar,” or “The God of the Sea,” emphasizing the temple’s deep spiritual connection to the region

Malvan Killa

Sindhudurg Fort is a historical sea fort located in Arabian Sea near the Konkan region of Maharashtra in Western India. The fort, commissioned by Chhatrapati Shivaji, was constructed between 1664 and 1667.The fort lies off the shore of Malvan taluka of the Sindhudurg District in the Konkan region of Maharashtra.Malvan Killa, officially known as Sindhudurg Fort, is a magnificent 17th-century sea fort built by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj on an islet off the coast of Malvan in Maharashtra's Konkan region, famous for its massive sea walls, hidden entrance, sweet water reservoirs, and as a recently designated UNESCO World Heritage Site, offering rich history and stunning coastal views.
